RSA Cryptography Basics

1. What Problem Does RSA Solve

Before public key cryptography:

  • Symmetric encryption requires sharing a secret key in advance
  • This is impractical on open networks

RSA is a classical public key cryptosystem.

2. Core Mathematical Idea

RSA relies on the following fact:

n=p⋅qn = p \cdot q n=p⋅q

Where:

  • ppp is a large prime
  • qqq is a large prime

Given ppp and qqq, computing nnn is easy.
Given nnn, factoring it into ppp and qqq is computationally hard.

3. Key Generation

Step 1: Choose primes

Choose two large primes:

p, qp,\ q p, q

Step 2: Compute modulus

n=p⋅qn = p \cdot q n=p⋅q

Step 3: Compute Euler function

φ(n)=(p−1)(q−1)\varphi(n) = (p - 1)(q - 1) φ(n)=(p−1)(q−1)

Step 4: Choose public exponent

Choose eee such that:

1<e<φ(n)1 < e < \varphi(n) 1<e<φ(n)

and

gcd(e,φ(n))=1\gcd(e, \varphi(n)) = 1 gcd(e,φ(n))=1

Step 5: Compute private exponent

Compute ddd such that:

e \cdot d \equiv 1 \pmod{\varphi(n)}

4. Encryption and Decryption

Encryption

Given plaintext mmm, compute ciphertext ccc:

c \equiv m^e \pmod{n}

Decryption

Recover plaintext mmm from ciphertext ccc:

m \equiv c^d \pmod{n}

5. Why Decryption Works

By Euler’s theorem:

m^{\varphi(n)} \equiv 1 \pmod{n}

Since:

e \cdot d \equiv 1 \pmod{\varphi(n)}

RSA decryption correctly recovers the original message.

6. Summary

  • RSA security relies on the difficulty of factoring large integers
  • Encryption: m^e \bmod n
  • Decryption: c^d \bmod n
